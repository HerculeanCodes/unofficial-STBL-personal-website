I Am Tim Berners-Lee

I am the founder of WWW (World Wide Web)





My full name is Sir Timothy John Berners-Lee, OM, KBE, FRS, FREng, FRSA, DFBCS, RDI (born 8 June 1955), also known as TimBL, was an English computer scientist best known as the inventor of the World Wide Web. I'm a Professorial Fellow of Computer Science at the University of Oxford and a professor at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT). I proposed an information management system on 12 March 1989, then implemented the first successful communication between a Hypertext Transfer Protocol (HTTP) client and server via the Internet in mid-November.

I'm the director of the the director of the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C), which oversees the continued development of the Web. I co-founded (with my then wife-to-be Rosemary Leith) the World Wide Web Foundation. I'm a senior researcher and holder of the 3Com founder's chair at the MIT Computer Science and Artificial Intelligence Laboratory (CSAIL). I'm a director of the Web Science Research Initiative (WSRI) and a member of the advisory board of the MIT Center for Collective Intelligence. In 2011, I was named as a member of the board of trustees of the Ford Foundation. I'm a founder and president of the Open Data Institute and is currently an advisor at social network MeWe.

I devised and implemented the first Web browser and Web server, and helped foster the Web's subsequent explosive development. I currently directs the W3 Consortium, developing tools and standards to further the Web's potential. In April 2009, I was elected as Foreign Associate of the National Academy of Sciences.

In 2004, I was knighted by Queen Elizabeth II for my pioneering work. I was named in Time magazine's list of the 100 Most Important People of the 20th century and has received a number of other accolades for my invention. I was honoured as the "Inventor of the World Wide Web" during the 2012 Summer Olympics opening ceremony in which I appeared working with a vintage NeXT Computer. I tweeted "This is for everyone" which appeared in LED lights attached to the chairs of the audience. I received the 2016 Turing Award "for inventing the World Wide Web, the first web browser, and the fundamental protocols and algorithms allowing the Web to scale"
